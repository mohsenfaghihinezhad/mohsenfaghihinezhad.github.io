Description of Competency

This competency focuses on creating a learning environment where students feel supported, respected, and encouraged to participate. For me, an effective learning environment is not only about presenting course content clearly, but also about building a classroom culture where students feel safe to ask questions, interact with each other, and connect the material to real problems.

In environmental engineering, students may come with different backgrounds, levels of preparation, and confidence in technical topics. Because of that, I think it is important to create a classroom environment that is clear, organized, inclusive, and engaging.

Artifact Rationales

I selected these artifacts because they show how my understanding of the classroom environment has developed. In my reflection worksheet, I focused on setting a good mindset before class, creating a safe environment where students feel comfortable asking questions, being transparent with materials and assessment, and showing students that I care about their learning.

The COLA workshop on student engagement helped me think about engagement in three different ways: student-to-instructor, student-to-student, and student-to-material. This was useful because it showed me that engagement is not only about whether students are listening to the instructor. It also includes whether students feel connected to each other and whether the material feels meaningful to them.

The inclusive pedagogy workshop also helped me think about who may be left behind in the classroom and how I can invite them back into the learning process. I found this idea very important because students may not participate for many reasons, and silence does not always mean that a student is not learning. It reminded me to be more careful about my assumptions and to create a classroom environment where students feel respected and included.

Interpretation

This competency helped me understand that an effective learning environment starts before the class begins. The instructor’s mindset, preparation, organization, and transparency can affect how students experience the course. Simple actions, such as arriving early, checking the classroom technology, explaining expectations clearly, and making materials accessible, can reduce confusion and help students focus on learning.

I also learned that creating an effective learning environment requires intentional engagement. In my future teaching, I would like to use more real-world examples, short interactive activities, polls, and collaborative discussions. These strategies can help students stay engaged and see how environmental engineering concepts connect to practical problems.

Another important takeaway is that inclusion should be part of the course environment from the beginning. I want to be more intentional about using inclusive language, learning and pronouncing students’ names correctly, avoiding assumptions about students’ backgrounds, and creating opportunities for students to participate in different ways.

Overall, this competency helped me see that effective teaching is not only about content knowledge. It is also about creating a space where students feel comfortable, motivated, and supported enough to engage with challenging material.
