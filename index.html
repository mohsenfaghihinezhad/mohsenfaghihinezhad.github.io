About

I am a Ph.D. student in Environmental Engineering at Michigan State University, where I work as a Graduate Research Assistant and have also served as a Graduate Teaching Assistant. My research focuses on biological and chemical approaches for water, wastewater, and groundwater remediation.

My current research investigates cometabolic biodegradation of chlorinated ethenes and 1,4-dioxane by methane- and propane-oxidizing microbial communities. I am broadly interested in developing sustainable and effective treatment strategies for environmental contaminants.

Research Interests

  • Bioremediation of environmental contaminants
  • Cometabolic biodegradation of chlorinated solvents
  • Methanotrophic and propanotrophic microbial communities
  • Environmental microbiology
  • Functional genes involved in contaminant biodegradation
  • Contaminants of emerging concern
  • Advanced oxidation processes, including catalytic ozonation, photocatalysis, and UV-H2O2
  • Adsorption processes for water and wastewater treatment
