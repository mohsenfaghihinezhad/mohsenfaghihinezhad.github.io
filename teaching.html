Overview

This teaching portfolio presents my development as an educator in environmental engineering. It includes my teaching philosophy, teaching experience, course design materials, workshop reflections, and evidence toward the Certificate in College Teaching.

This portfolio also supports my second deliverable for the COLA program by organizing my teaching-related work, reflections, and future teaching goals in one place.

Teaching Interests

My teaching interests include environmental engineering, environmental chemistry, water and wastewater treatment, emerging contaminants, advanced treatment systems, and bioremediation. I am especially interested in helping students connect technical concepts to real environmental problems.

Certification in College Teaching

This section organizes materials that demonstrate my progress toward the Certificate in College Teaching. Each competency includes a description, related course or experience, artifacts, artifact rationales, and reflection.

Competency 1: Approved Course in College Teaching

This competency is based on completing EGR 811: Foundations of Engineering Education. My artifacts include a course redesign project for ENE 280: Principles of Environmental Engineering and Science, including a syllabus, 15-week course plan, lecture materials, in-class activity, homework rubric, final report, and presentation.

COLA Program Deliverables

As part of the COLA program, I am developing teaching-related deliverables and receiving feedback from my cohort and group mentor. My teaching philosophy statement serves as one deliverable, and this teaching portfolio is being developed as another deliverable.

Teaching materials included in this portfolio are connected to environmental engineering education and course design. Current materials include examples from my ENE 280 course redesign project, including emerging contaminants, advanced oxidation processes, and biologically mediated remediation.

Teaching Experience
