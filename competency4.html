Description of Competency

This competency focuses on understanding how teaching is shaped by the type, mission, and expectations of an institution. For me, this competency was important because teaching does not happen in isolation. The way instructors design courses, support students, assess learning, and balance teaching with research or service can depend strongly on the university context.

Understanding the university context helps me think more carefully about the kind of institution I may work in, the students I may teach, and how I can adapt my teaching to different expectations and learning environments.

Workshop/Experience Completed

Understanding the University Context: Aligning Teaching with Different Institutional Types and Missions
Certification in College Teaching Institute
Michigan State University

In this session, I reflected on how universities can differ in their missions, values, student populations, and faculty responsibilities. This helped me think about how teaching may look different at a research-intensive university, comprehensive public university, liberal arts college, minority-serving institution, or community college.

Artifacts and Materials Developed

For this competency, I used the following materials as evidence of my development:

  • Reflection worksheet from the CCT session on Understanding the University Context
  • Institutional mission statement and teaching statement handout
  • Comparison of different institutional types and expectations for teaching
  • Reflection on how institutional context can shape inclusive teaching and career fit

Artifact Rationales

I selected these artifacts because they show how I started to think about teaching beyond the individual classroom. Before this session, I mostly thought about teaching in terms of course content, classroom activities, and student engagement. This competency helped me see that institutional mission also matters.

One of my main takeaways was that each university has different responsibilities and expectations for faculty members. For example, some institutions may place more emphasis on research, while others may focus more strongly on undergraduate teaching, community engagement, or student support. Knowing these differences can help me better understand what kind of institution I am entering and how I may need to adapt.

I also selected these materials because they connect directly to my future career planning. As I think about postdoctoral, faculty, or teaching-related positions, I need to consider not only the research fit, but also whether the institution’s teaching mission and student population fit my background, goals, and teaching style.

Interpretation

This competency helped me understand that effective teaching should be connected to the institution where it takes place. A course that works well in one setting may need to be adjusted in another setting depending on class size, student preparation, institutional mission, available resources, and expectations for faculty.

One question I am still thinking about is whether I can adapt myself to a wide range of institutional expectations, or whether I should focus more on institutions that fit my background, interests, and career goals. This is important for me because I want my future teaching and research to support each other rather than feel disconnected.

I also connected this competency to inclusive teaching. Students do not all come from the same background, and institutions do not all serve the same student populations. In my future teaching, I want to keep this in mind and adjust my teaching approach based on the students, classroom, and institutional context.

Overall, this competency helped me see that being an effective instructor requires more than knowing the subject and preparing good course materials. It also requires understanding the mission of the institution, the needs of the students, and the role that teaching plays within that larger academic environment.
